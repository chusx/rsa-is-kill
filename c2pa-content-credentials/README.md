# c2pa-content-credentials — RSA in AI-generated media provenance

**Standard:** C2PA 2.x (Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity)
**Industry:** AI-generated media watermarking, news/journalism, creative tooling
**Algorithm:** RSA-2048/3072/4096 (claim signatures, X.509 chains), Ed25519/ECDSA permitted

## What it does

C2PA Content Credentials attach a tamper-evident provenance manifest to every
piece of digital media. An embedded JUMBF (JPEG Universal Metadata Box Format)
block inside an image/video carries a signed manifest describing who created it,
which tool produced it, whether AI was involved (`c2pa.action.ai_generated` or
`c2pa.action.ai_edited`), and what edits have happened since creation.

The signature is a COSE Sign1 over the manifest claim, by an X.509 certificate
chaining to a C2PA-recognized CA. Every major AI image generator ships C2PA
manifests today:

- **OpenAI** DALL-E 3, GPT-4o image generation, Sora — all outputs carry C2PA
- **Microsoft** Designer, Copilot image creator, Bing Image Creator
- **Adobe** Firefly, Photoshop Generative Fill
- **Google** SynthID + C2PA on Imagen outputs
- **Meta AI** image generator
- **Leica M11-P**, **Sony α1 II**, **Nikon Z9** — in-camera C2PA signing
- **BBC**, **New York Times**, **AFP**, **Reuters** — editorial provenance

The CA ecosystem is young. DigiCert, GlobalSign, and IdenTrust run C2PA-issuing
sub-CAs. The C2PA trust list (equivalent to a CAB Forum root store) is
maintained by the C2PA consortium and consumed by verifier implementations.

## Why it's stuck

- C2PA 2.0 (2024) mandates COSE Sign1 with RFC 8152 algorithms. RSA PKCS#1 v1.5
  (`RS256`/`RS384`/`RS512`) and RSA-PSS (`PS256`/`PS384`/`PS512`) are in the
  approved algorithm list alongside ES256/ES384/ES512. Deployed signing fleets
  at Adobe, Microsoft, OpenAI predominantly use RSA (HSM-backed code-signing
  style keys).
- Content Credentials are embedded in files at creation time and are expected
  to remain verifiable for the lifetime of the media. A 2024-signed AI image
  indexed into a newsroom archive must verify in 2040.
- C2PA time-stamping uses RFC 3161 TSA countersignatures — see
  `rfc3161-tsa-timestamp/`. Chain of trust compounds.
- The trust list update mechanism is centralized (signed list published by
  C2PA), but the list itself is signed with RSA.

## impact

- **AI attribution collapse**: The entire point of C2PA for AI images is to
  distinguish "this was generated by DALL-E" from "this is a photograph of a
  real event." Factor an AI-image-issuer's RSA key and attackers forge
  C2PA manifests that label synthetic deepfakes as "captured by a Leica" with
  a valid-looking provenance trail.
- **Newsroom evidence forgery**: BBC, NYT, Reuters use C2PA to prove a video
  came from their own reporters' devices. A forged Reuters signing cert
  produces "authentic press footage" of events that never happened.
- **Platform trust-label bypass**: Meta, YouTube, LinkedIn, TikTok are rolling
  out C2PA-aware "AI-generated" labels. Forged signatures remove or alter the
  AI-generated label, or conversely add it to genuine journalism to discredit
  it.
- **Camera authenticity forgery**: Leica/Sony/Nikon camera-level C2PA signing
  is marketed as hardware-of-trust. Factoring the camera manufacturer's signing
  CA means arbitrary images appear as if captured by a specific physical camera
  at a specific time.
- **Regulatory non-compliance**: The EU AI Act Article 50 (2024) requires
  marking of AI-generated content. C2PA is the leading technical mechanism.
  A factoring attack removes the mark.
- **Deepfake election influence**: 2024-2028 election cycles rely on C2PA
  increasingly as the "provenance ground truth" for fact-checkers. Forged
  provenance turns that ground truth into weaponized disinformation.

## Code

- `c2pa_manifest_sign.py` — build a C2PA claim, sign it as COSE Sign1 with an
  RSA-2048 PS256 key, embed the JUMBF block into a JPEG.
- `c2pa_verify.py` — parse a JPEG, extract the JUMBF manifest, verify the COSE
  signature against the C2PA trust list, walk the ingredient chain.
- `c2pa_claim_schema.json` — example AI-generator claim (assertions for
  `c2pa.actions`, `c2pa.ingredient`, `stds.schema-org.CreativeWork`).
